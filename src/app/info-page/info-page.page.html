Information Guide Weather Info Guide

🌡️ Temperature

Measured in degrees Celsius. This is the ambient air temperature around you.

💧 Humidity

The percentage of water vapor in the air. High humidity can make it feel hotter.

📈 Pressure

Measured in hPa. Low pressure usually brings rain, while high pressure means clearer skies.

💨 Wind

Speed is in m/s, and direction in degrees (0° = North). It affects how the temperature feels.

🌫️ Visibility

Measured in kilometers. Lower values mean fog, heavy rain, or other obstructions.

☁️ Cloud Coverage

Percentage of the sky covered by clouds. 100% means fully overcast.

🧠 How Weather Works

What Causes Rain?

Warm air rises and cools, causing water vapor to condense into droplets. When droplets get heavy, they fall as rain.

 Why Does Wind Blow?

It's all about air pressure. Air moves from high-pressure areas to low-pressure areas. The bigger the difference, the stronger the wind.

 What Are Clouds Made Of?

Clouds are made of tiny water droplets or ice crystals suspended in the air. They form when warm, moist air rises, cools, and condenses around small particles the droplets can cling to like dust or pollen.

 How Is Temperature Measured?

Temperature is measured using a thermometer, which detects how hot or cold something is based on the expansion of a liquid (like mercury or alcohol) or electronic sensors. It reflects the average kinetic energy - or movement - of the molecules in a substance.

 What Is Thunder?

Thunder is the sound produced by the rapid expansion and contraction of air caused by lightning's extreme heat.

 What Causes A Tornado?

A tornado forms when warm, moist air meets cold, dry air, causing strong winds to rotate and form a funnel.

 What Is A Cold Front?

A cold front occurs when a mass of cold air pushes into warmer air, often bringing heavy rain or storms.

 What Is A Warm Front?

A warm front happens when warm air slides over cooler air, typically bringing light rain or cloudy weather.
